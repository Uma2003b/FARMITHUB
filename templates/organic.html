Natural Fertilizers & Pest Control

üåæ Organic Fertilizers

  • Compost: Mix kitchen waste, crop residue, and manure. Ready in 3-4 months.
  • Vermicompost: Use earthworms to convert waste. Rich in nutrients and microbes.
  • Farmyard Manure: Traditional and effective. Apply 5-10 tonnes per hectare.
  • Green Manure: Grow legumes and plow them back into soil for nitrogen.
  • Biofertilizers: Azospirillum and Phosphobacteria improve nutrient availability.

üõ°Ô∏è Natural Pest Control

  • Neem Oil: Mix 5% neem oil with water. Spray on affected plants every 7-10 days.
  • Garlic Spray: Blend garlic with water. Repels aphids and mites.
  • Companion Planting: Grow marigold, basil near vegetables to repel pests.
  • Beneficial Insects: Ladybugs and lacewings eat harmful pests naturally.
  • Crop Rotation: Change crop location yearly to break pest cycles.

Organic Crops & Best Practices

üçö Rice (SRI Method)

  • Transplant young seedlings (8-12 days)
  • Wide spacing for better growth
  • Intermittent wetting saves water
  • Higher yield with less water

üåæ Wheat

  • Sow in November-December
  • Use farmyard manure + biofertilizers
  • Minimal pest issues in winter
  • Harvest in March-April

ü´ò Pulses (Dal)

Transition to Organic Farming

üìã 3-Year Transition Plan

  • Year 1: Stop using synthetic chemicals. Start composting. Build soil with organic matter.
  • Year 2: Implement crop rotation. Use biofertilizers. Monitor soil improvement.
  • Year 3: Apply for organic certification. Document all practices. Achieve premium prices.

üí° Quick Tips for Success

  • Start small - convert 1-2 acres first to learn
  • Join organic farmer groups for support and knowledge sharing
  • Keep detailed records of all activities and inputs
  • Connect with organic markets before certification
  • Be patient - soil health improves over time
  • Use local resources - compost, manure, natural pest control
